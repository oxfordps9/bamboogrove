Our Story

A grove of bamboo, a fire that never goes out.

How a roadside grill beside Al-Manzar View Point became one of Hyderabad's favourite family dining spots.

The Idea

Grill culture, grown under bamboo

Bamboo Grove opened on Jamshoro Road with a simple brief: build the kind of place Hyderabad's grill-lovers would drive out to on purpose. Not a food-street stall, not a formal dining room — something in between, where the charcoal smoke is part of the atmosphere and the bamboo screens do the rest.

The location beside Al-Manzar View Point wasn't an accident. It gave the restaurant room to breathe: an open-air courtyard, tall bamboo dividers between seating clusters, and a skyline that turns gold right around the time the first skewers hit the grill.

What started as a grill-first menu has grown into a kitchen that also plates Arabic mezze, Afghan mandi, and Peshawari-style karahi — a menu shaped by Hyderabad's own mix of Sindhi, Pakhtun, and Gulf-influenced tastes.

What Guides the Kitchen

Three things we won't compromise on

Fire over shortcuts

Everything that can go on charcoal, goes on charcoal — cooked to order, not held under a lamp.

Built for the family table

Platters are portioned for sharing, and the courtyard seating is arranged for groups, not just couples.

A room worth the drive

Bamboo screens, lantern light, and the view toward Al-Manzar — the setting matters as much as the plate.
